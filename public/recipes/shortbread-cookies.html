Shortbread Cookies

Introduction

Welcome to the classic world of shortbread cookies! This recipe will guide you through the process of creating these buttery, crumbly treats that are perfect for any occasion. Whether you're a seasoned baker or new to the kitchen, these cookies are simple to make and utterly delicious. Let's get started on this timeless Scottish delight!

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
All-purpose flour 2 cups (240g)
Unsalted butter, room temperature 1 cup (226g)
Granulated sugar 1/2 cup (100g)
Vanilla extract 1 tsp (5ml)
Salt 1/4 tsp (1.5g)

Cooking Time

Preparation Time: 15 minutes

Cooking Time: 20-25 minutes

Total Time: 35-40 minutes

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the unsalted butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Add the vanilla extract and salt, and mix until well combined.
  4. Gradually add the all-purpose flour to the butter mixture and mix until a dough forms.
  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/2 inch thickness.
  6. Use a cookie cutter to cut out shapes and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Prick each cookie a few times with a fork to prevent puffing during baking.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.
  9. Remove from the oven and let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Tips

  • Chill the dough: If the dough is too soft or sticky, wrap it in plastic wrap and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before rolling it out.
  • Even thickness: To ensure even baking, try to roll out the dough to a uniform thickness.
  • Flavor variations: Feel free to add lemon zest, orange zest, or ground spices like cinnamon or nutmeg to the dough for a different flavor profile.
  • Storage: Store the cooled shortbread cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week.
  • Decorating: Once cooled, you can dip the cookies in melted chocolate or drizzle them with icing for a decorative touch.
